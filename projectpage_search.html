Adapted Search experience

Intuitive and efficient search journeys by adapting to user intents and product categories.

Visit ikea.com
Project Date 2021/22
Client/team IKEA
Skills UX research
UX/UI
Product strategy
Workshop facilitation



Background

This projects was part of a bigger strategic effort to secure the ground prior to the transport crisis and health crisis in coming years.
From a business perspective, smaller profit margins and more prominent competition.
From user perspective, "easy of shopping" is key, while availability and affordability are increasing painpoints.



Exploration

We observed customers visit ikea.com with different purposes. Also, our standard search experience was efficient in meeting the needs of those "simple" product-focus searches, but other intents, not as good.




br>

Problem definition

The learnings were destiled in a search-intents model(1), and confirmed later with a triangulation of different research techniques (2) in collaboration with other UX coworkers.
The learnings were also used by data science and engineering to train the search algorithm to predict intents.


Change of Focus

From one-time visit transactional relationships to relationships that last over time.




Ideation phase

I plan and facilitated ideation workshops including SMEs, SEO and the team to ideate and prioritise work.



Prototyping and testing

Work was prioritised based on business and user impact and feasability.
I enjoy involving engineers from the very begining of the prototyping phase. Great ideas are co-created and potential technical blockers discovered.
When testing, we agreed on using a triangulation of remote qualitative tecniques and AB testing.
